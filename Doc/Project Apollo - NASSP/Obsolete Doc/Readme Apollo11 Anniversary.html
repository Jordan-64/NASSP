ORBITER

Apollo 11 34th Anniversary 3D-Mesh Pack for Orbiter with NASSP v4.xx

by Rodion M. Herrera (rodion@my.msuiit.edu.ph)

INTRODUCTION

The fifth manned Apollo mission (and the one NASA declared was to make the first lunar landing) was launched on July 16, 1969. Four days later, on July 20, 1969, Apollo 11 Commander Neil Armstrong and Lunar Module Pilot Edwin "Buzz" Aldrin landed within a pre-designated elliptical landing area, on the Sea of Tranquility. This was the very first time that human beings landed on the surface of a heavenly body other than that of our Planet Earth. During the final approach, Armstrong recognized West Crater right at pitchover and in fact, prolonged the landing a bit so that he could avoid landing in it. He touched down in a very flat and level area and only after a few hours of preparation and going over the EVA checklists, Neil Armstrong went out of the LM hatch, descended the ladder, and became the first human being to ever walk on the moon.

Aldrin followed him down the ladder a few minutes later, and for the next two hours they explored what Buzz referred to as the "magnificent desolation" of the lunar surface immediately around the LM. The EVA being broadcasted live around the globe, the whole world watched in awe and wonder as the two astronauts explored and set up experiments. People who remember that historical broadcast still recall vividly the inspiring and proud feeling that human beings have now truly touched another world and that the first tiny steps in humanity's feeble attempts to conquer the stars has begun.

THE ADD-ON

This add-on simply changes the default NASSP v4.xx LM meshes, the astronaut (lunar EVA) mesh, and the US Flag mesh with some detailed and Apollo 11 specific meshes. I wanted to come up with this add-on because today, July 16, 2003, marks the 34th anniversary of the Apollo 11 launch, and I wanted those Orbiter and NASSP fans who are currently simulating the AP11 flight and lunar landing to have a more or less accurate 3D-representation of the AP11 LM and astronaul Neil Armstrong, when he took is first step on the lunar surface. Take note that this add-on:

1. DOES NOT provide any new scenery mesh. You have to download and configure Jtiberius' Tranquility Base scenery files for Orbiter in order to have an AP11 landing site.

2. DOES NOT alter any of the NASSP modules.

3. DOES NOT come with other fancy LEVA enhancements. It's just simply Neil and the US Flag (if I have more time I might add Buzz, the T.V. Cam and antenna, and the SWC experiment later).

INSTALLATION

IMPORTANT: Although I haven't tried the addon in older versions of Orbiter, IT IS ASSUMED THAT THIS ADD-ON WILL ONLY WORK WITH ORBITER BUILD 020212 (with 030303 patch)

REMINDER #1: If for some reason you want to preserve your current NASSP LM and or Astronaut 3D-model, then BACK-UP THE FOLLOWING FILES :

1. ../Orbiter/Meshes/sat5AstroS.msh

2. ..\Orbiter\Meshes\LM_ascent.msh

3. ..\Orbiter\Meshes\LM_ascent2.msh

4. ..\Orbiter\Meshes\LM_descent.msh

5. ..\Orbiter\Meshes\LM_NoWheel.msh

5. ..\Orbiter\Meshes\LM_Parked.msh

5. ..\Orbiter\Meshes\LM_vessel_on.msh

5. ..\Orbiter\Meshes\usflag.msh

REMINDER #2: You must have the following addons installed in this order:

1. NASSPv4.1 or v4.2

2. Mare Tranquility landing site by Jtiberius

REMINDER #3: Optional files to install :

1. OrbiterSound by DanSteph

2. P64 and Rabid Dogs' CSM/LM panel (Apollo Custom Panel 3.0a Final...or something like that)

Simply unzip the package into your ORBITER folder. If prompted with "Overwrite?" respond with a YES.

IMPORTANT NOTES:

1. The new LM has landing probes sticking out from underneath the footpads. Now, I've noticed that when landing at Jtiberius' Tranquility mesh, the LM seems to be NOT touching the surface mesh, making it appear to float in the air a bit. The astronaut does this float thing too. I will ask permission from Kopp if I could nudge his mesh a bit upwards so it comes into contact with the LM footpads and release it as a fix later.

2. Due to what I believe is the high polycount of the new LM, there seems to be a delay/freeze when you press the button to extract the LM from the SIVB. Don't worry, the extraction will still take place in a few seconds and you may still be able to catch a glimpse of the SIVB drifting away when you press F1 (external view).
